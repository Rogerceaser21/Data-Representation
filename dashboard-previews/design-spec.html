01

Direction

Cinematic. A dark, deep, alive instrument. The spectacle lives in the backdrop, the entry, and the transitions; the data views themselves stay dense and legible. We borrow the cinematic components you liked (the glow on the rating colours, the depth, the big display serif, the signature backdrop) and pull them across both themes. This is the language for every board.

02

The signature: an AIS star-field

The backdrop you are looking at right now. A slow-drifting field of stars in the four AIS colours only (yellow, blue, red, green), with gentle pointer parallax and a soft twinkle, sitting behind the data. It is the one three.js signature moment, reused as ambient depth, never on top of the numbers. It is toggleable on or off from the masthead, independent of the theme, so it can be calmed down for a formal governor session and turned up for a launch.

  • Stars, not dots. Five-point AIS-coloured stars, the round constellation dots are gone.
  • AIS palette only. No off-brand colours in the field.
  • Performance-safe. Capped pixel ratio, paused when the tab is hidden or the toggle is off.
Tune the field, then we lock it
03

The entry, and how it moves

The first three seconds

On login, an authoritative title-card holds for a beat over the star-field (AIS crest, "Observation Record, Term 3"), then dissolves as the data assembles itself: numbers count up, the matrix fills, the gauge sweeps. The star depth recedes as the data takes the foreground. Authority first, then life.

Board to board

Shared-element motion is the grammar. Click a teacher and their row flies and grows into their page; the school gauge morphs into a teacher gauge; nothing redraws, everything moves. One light camera-depth push carries the login into the first board. Count-ups and staggered reveals throughout.

04

The rating language

The official 6-point SEAS scale, the exact language SEAS grades you in and what sits in the database. Best evidence wins (the strongest observation stands). "Not Assessed" is a neutral state, not a score.

05

06

Theme

Decided

The dashboard opens in light (paper) by default, with dark a tap away. Both themes are first-class and the AIS star-field works in either. The Cinematic language (the glow, the depth, the big serif, the backdrop) carries across both. This spec page now opens light to match.

08

Data and attendance

Source of record
  • The dashboard reads only its own Supabase (governance DB), already holding 312 observations and 486 scores.
  • Ratings on the 6-point scale, best evidence wins.
  • Records are immutable evidence; notes and SEF tags are attributed, timestamped additions.
Attendance, as a governance metric
  • Rollups at whole-school, Primary, Secondary, and Grade, never per-student here.
  • A link out to the Attendance App for the granular detail.
  • Pulled as a snapshot now, moving to the hub Supabase later.
